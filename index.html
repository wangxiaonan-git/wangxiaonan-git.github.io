About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computational Linguistics at Yonsei University, advised by Professor Hansaem Kim. As a researcher at the Data & Resource Convergence Lab, my research focuses on enhancing the cultural and security alignment of LLMs by investigating evaluative methodologies to ensure privacy protection and linguistic reliability across diverse cultural and dialectal contexts.

Research Interests

My research lies broadly in Natural Language Processing (NLP), with a particular focus on multilingual safety alignment, privacy-preserving language modeling, and cross-cultural evaluation. I am especially interested in how LLMs acquire and deploy culturally-specific knowledge, the internal mechanisms underlying cross-lingual generalization failures, and the development of robust diagnostic benchmarks for privacy and security.

Feel free to reach out for collaborations on NLP, AI Safety, and Multilingual Alignment, including privacy preservation, computational linguistics, and their intersections with social sciences and data security.
