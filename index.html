About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computational Linguistics at Yonsei University, advised by Professor Hansaem Kim. As a researcher at the Data & Resource Convergence Lab, my research focuses on enhancing the cultural and security alignment of LLMs by investigating evaluative methodologies to ensure privacy protection and linguistic reliability across diverse cultural and dialectal contexts.

Research Interests

My research lies broadly in Natural Language Processing (NLP), with a particular interest in multilingual safety alignment, privacy-preserving LLMs, and culturally-aware evaluation. I am especially interested in how LLMs represent and protect culturally specific sensitive information, how their internal mechanisms fail in non-English contexts, and how these vulnerabilities can be diagnosed through error-driven benchmarking.

  • LLM Security and Privacy: Recognizing culturally specific PII (Wang et al., 2025), diagnosing fine-grained failures in name recognition (K-NameDiag), and mitigating privacy risks in data generation.
  • Multilingual Benchmarking: Developing evaluative methodologies for Korean-specific contexts, investigating cross-lingual knowledge transfer, and constructing dialectal benchmarks (NorthKoreanEval).
  • Cultural Alignment: Assessing LLMs' understanding of cultural specificities and idioms, and exploring human-inspired linguistic reliability across diverse social contexts.
  • Efficient NLP Methods: Prompt-based diagnostics, automated dataset construction via APIs (CuisineFlow), and memory-augmented multimodal representation.

Feel free to reach out if you're interested in topics related to NLP, LLM Safety, and Evaluation Benchmarks, including multilinguality, privacy protection, computational linguistics, and their intersections with social sciences, cultural anthropology, and data security.
